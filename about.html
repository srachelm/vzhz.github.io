About

Veronica came to software engineering from the earth sciences, where she gained research experience in both academia and industry. There she got her first taste of object-oriented programming. She has worked in a geochemistry and paleomagnetism lab and contributed to landing-site selection for the Mars Curiosity Rover . After these projects, she began working on Python full-time, recently devoting three months to a deep dive into learning the language and general programming principles at the Recurse Center.

She hopes, one day, to be able to squeeze as much information out of large datasets as she learned to squeeze out of rocks. In the meantime, she is working on making the best personal website ever from the ground up and building a web app in Flask. Her other projects include some (rather addictive) terminal games and the Friendly Terminal tool.

Interests: backpacking, climbing, biking, piloting planes, science outreach, and finding new and unusual ingredients to put on pizza
